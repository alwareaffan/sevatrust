Serving Humanity with Compassion

Seva Charitable Trust is a registered non governmental organization dedicated to social welfare, education support, women empowerment, medical assistance and community upliftment.

About Seva Charitable Trust

Our Background

Seva Charitable Trust was established in the year 2024 and is registered under the Mumbai Public Trust Act, 1950. The trust is committed to serving society with honesty, transparency and compassion.

Our Mission

To uplift underprivileged communities by providing social support, education assistance, healthcare aid and empowerment opportunities with dignity and care.

Our Vision

To build a compassionate and inclusive society where every individual has access to basic needs, education and opportunities for growth.

Our Work & Initiatives

Education support for underprivileged children
Women empowerment and skill development programs
Medical aid and healthcare assistance
Community welfare and social awareness initiatives

Activities & Impact Stories

A timeline of our initiatives showcasing real impact created at the grassroots level. Each activity reflects our commitment to service, dignity, and transparency.

January 2024

Community Support Drive

Essential ration and daily necessities were distributed to economically weaker families, ensuring food security and immediate relief.

Impact: Supported multiple families with dignity and care.

March 2024

Education Assistance Program

Educational materials and guidance were provided to underprivileged students to prevent school dropouts and encourage learning.

Impact: Helped students continue their education with confidence.

June 2024

Medical Assistance & Awareness

Medical support and health awareness initiatives were conducted to assist individuals facing healthcare challenges.

Impact: Improved awareness and access to basic medical support.

September 2024

Women & Social Empowerment

Awareness and empowerment initiatives focused on women rights, dignity, and self reliance.

Impact: Encouraged confidence and social participation among women.
